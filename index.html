PRODUCT 01

FLUXENGINE™

"We don't predict your grid's limit. We generate 10,000 parallel universes and find the MW hiding in every one."

FLUXENGINE™ — Probabilistic Interconnection Intelligence

THE PROBLEM

The US interconnection queue holds 1,400 GW of generation and 890 GW of storage — more than twice the country's total installed capacity. Only 13% of projects entering the queue ever reach commercial operation. The median wait: 4+ years. Traditional interconnection studies run one deterministic scenario. One snapshot. One answer. Developers wait 18 months for a number that may not reflect reality when their project actually builds.

THE SOLUTION — TWO COMPONENTS

FLUXGEN: A Score-Based Diffusion Model with Kirchhoff-Constrained scenario generation. Instead of one operating point, FluxGen generates 10,000+ synthetic 8,760-hour scenarios statistically indistinguishable from reality — including correlated extreme events (heatwave + solar ramp + wind drought simultaneously) that traditional studies miss entirely.

FLUXFLOW: A Heterogeneous Graph Neural Network power flow surrogate that validates each scenario 1,000× faster than Newton-Raphson. Where PSS/E takes 50-100ms per solution, FluxFlow takes 0.05-0.1ms. At 87.6 million scenario-hours, that difference is the difference between weeks and minutes.

PRODUCT 02

GRIDGUARD-GNN

"We don't just find the needle in the haystack — we find the neighborhood conspiring to hide it."

GRIDGUARD-GNN — Revenue Assurance & Loss Analytics

THE PROBLEM

$96 billion is stolen from electricity utilities every year through meter tampering, billing fraud, and illegal connections. In emerging markets — India, Brazil, South Africa, Southeast Asia — non-technical loss rates reach 20-40% of total generation. Traditional detection treats every meter as an independent time series. It misses the pattern entirely.

The flaw: electricity theft is a network phenomenon. When a customer bypasses their meter, the energy balance of their entire transformer cluster breaks down. The theft is written in the topology — not in any single meter's data.

THE SOLUTION — ST-GAT ARCHITECTURE

GridGuard-GNN models the entire distribution network as a graph. Every meter is a node. Every transformer connection is an edge.

SPATIAL: GAT layers aggregate consumption across electrically adjacent meters. Attention weights learn which neighbors are most informative for detecting energy balance violations. A tampered meter in a cluster of 40 cannot hide — the imbalance between what the transformer supplies and what downstream meters report exposes it.

TEMPORAL: GRU layers capture the daily and weekly consumption rhythms of each customer type. Theft has a temporal signature too — the flat line of a bypassed meter, the sudden drop when a connection is made.

EXPLAINABILITY

Every flagged meter comes with:

  • → SHAP feature importance showing which consumption patterns drove the flag
  • → GAT attention weight visualization showing which neighbors contributed
  • → Neighbor comparison chart: flagged meter vs transformer cluster average
  • → Energy balance summary: transformer supply vs downstream meter readings

A revenue protection analyst reads each explanation card in 30 seconds.

PRODUCT 03

GRIDCAST-ST

"We don't forecast the sun — we forecast the shadow, station by station, minute by minute."

GRIDCAST-ST — Spatio-Temporal Renewable Intelligence

THE PROBLEM

A cloud shadow moving at 60 km/h across a solar farm can drop generation by 200 MW in 8 minutes. A wind ramp event can add or remove 300 MW from a regional grid in 15 minutes. The forecasting tools most grid operators use today treat each generation site independently. A single-station model predicting solar output at Farm A has no knowledge that a cloud shadow just crossed Farm B — 80km upwind — 12 minutes ago. That shadow is coming. The model doesn't know. This costs the industry hundreds of millions annually in unnecessary reserve costs and avoidable imbalance penalties.

THE SOLUTION — GRAPH-TRANSFORMER ARCHITECTURE

THE GRAPH COMPONENT: A Spatio-Temporal GNN connects nearby generation stations as graph nodes. GCN layers capture stable, geography-driven spatial correlations — terrain, altitude, coastal proximity. This is the map of stable relationships.

THE TRANSFORMER COMPONENT: A Gated Transformer with time-lagged attention captures dynamic, weather-driven interactions. When a cloud shadow crosses Station A, the model knows Station B (80km southeast, in the prevailing wind direction) will experience the same shadow in approximately 12 minutes.

THE SATELLITE LAYER: GOES-R satellite imagery feeds a Swin Transformer vision encoder that detects developing cloud formations before they affect any ground station. The system sees what is coming before any sensor on the ground can measure it.

RAMP EVENT DETECTION

A dedicated classification head predicts whether generation will change by more than 10% of installed capacity in any 15-minute window within the next 30 minutes. When a ramp alert fires, grid operators receive: predicted ramp magnitude in MW, timing window, confidence level, and recommended reserve pre-positioning action.

Quantum-Inspired Local Tensor Regression

Shetty Nihal Naveen, Ashil Shetty, B Karthik Udupa

Sahyadri College of Engineering and Management, Mangaluru

Published: Research Square, 2026

Riemannian Geometry Quantum Information Tensor Regression

What it is

QILTR recasts local tensor regression as a Riemannian optimization problem on the manifold of quantum states. By replacing standard Euclidean distance kernels with the Bures distance — derived from Quantum Fisher Information — QILTR achieves better-conditioned local Hessians and improved solution quality on multi-linear systems.

Key findings

  • → 4.9× improvement in outlier robustness
  • → +1.22% solution quality improvement on QM9 quantum chemistry benchmark
  • → Polynomial-time complexity — computationally tractable for real systems
  • → Formal convergence proof under local geodesic convexity

Relevance to Grid Genesis

The information geometry framework informs the uncertainty quantification layer in FluxEngine, where power system state spaces exhibit the same non-Euclidean geometry that motivated the Bures distance approach.

AMODO-EO: Adaptive Multi-Objective Drug Optimization with Emergent Objectives

Shetty Nihal Naveen

Drug Discovery Multi-Objective Optimization Symbolic Regression

What it is

AMODO-EO discovers and integrates new chemically meaningful objectives during active drug optimization — using symbolic regression and automated feature engineering to find emergent trade-offs invisible to the original objective set.

Key findings

  • → Consistently identified HBA/RTB ratio, MW/TPSA ratio, LogP × aromatic ring count as emergent objectives
  • → Pareto front expanded into higher-dimensional chemical spaces
  • → Only 1.5-2.9% hypervolume reduction vs baseline NSGA-II
  • → Ablation studies confirmed statistical filtering is essential

Broader significance

The adaptive objective discovery framework — finding what you should be optimizing for that you did not know to look for — is a general principle that informs how Grid Genesis approaches energy system optimization.
