About Grid Genesis

"We build the intelligence layer that governs energy systems too complex for human intuition alone."

Grid Genesis builds physics-informed AI at the intersection of power systems engineering, Riemannian geometry, and probabilistic machine learning. We see the electricity grid not as a collection of isolated data points — but as a living graph, where every node, every edge, and every flow of electrons is connected to everything else. That topology is where the intelligence lives.

Our three products address the three most quantifiable inefficiencies in the global energy transition: $96 billion lost annually to electricity theft, 1,400 gigawatts of clean energy trapped in interconnection queues, and hundreds of millions in avoidable renewable imbalance costs caused by inadequate forecasting.

Each product is built on the same foundation — graph neural networks that respect physics, probabilistic models that quantify uncertainty honestly, and a strict principle that humans always make the final decision.

Founded by researchers from Sahyadri College of Engineering and Management, Mangaluru, Grid Genesis emerged from published work in quantum information geometry, adaptive multi-objective optimization, and spatio-temporal forecasting. We are not incrementally improving existing tools. We are replacing the mathematical foundations they are built on.
